Lesson 2: Introductions

Let's learn how to introduce ourselves!

In this lesson, you will learn how to ask for someone's name, say your own name, and ask where they are from. We will also learn about the important difference between formal and informal address.

[မြန်မာဘာသာဖြင့် ရှင်းလင်းချက်]

Formal vs. Informal: "Sie" and "du"

In German, there are two ways to say "you". This is a very important concept.

  • Sie (formal "you"): Use "Sie" when talking to adults you don't know, in professional situations, with people in authority (like police officers or teachers), or to show respect to older people. The verb form used with "Sie" is the infinitive (e.g., "kommen", "heißen"). "Sie" is always capitalized.
  • du (informal "you"): Use "du" with friends, family members, children, and people your own age in casual situations. The verb form changes for "du" (e.g., "kommst", "heißt").

When you are learning, it is safest to use "Sie" unless someone invites you to use "du".

Asking for a Name

German English Formality
Wie heißen Sie?What is your name?Formal
Wie heißt du?What is your name?Informal

Stating Your Name

German English
Ich heiße ...My name is ...
Mein Name ist ...My name is ...

Asking Where Someone is From

German English Formality
Woher kommen Sie?Where do you come from?Formal
Woher kommst du?Where do you come from?Informal

Stating Where You Are From

German English
Ich komme aus Myanmar.I come from Myanmar.
Ich komme aus Deutschland.I come from Germany.

Example Dialogue

Here is a short formal dialogue between two people, A and B.

A: Guten Tag.

B: Guten Tag.

A: Wie heißen Sie?

B: Ich heiße Klaus. Und Sie?

A: Mein Name ist Maria. Woher kommen Sie, Herr Klaus?

B: Ich komme aus Deutschland. Und Sie?

A: Ich komme aus Österreich.
