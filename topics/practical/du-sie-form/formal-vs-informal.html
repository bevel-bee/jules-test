Formal vs. Informal "You": Sie and du

In German, there are two ways to say "you". This is a very important concept to understand for speaking politely.

The Formal "Sie"

Use "Sie" when talking to:

  • Adults you don't know
  • People in positions of authority (e.g., police, teachers)
  • In professional or business situations
  • To show respect to older people

The verb form used with "Sie" is the infinitive (the basic form, e.g., "kommen", "heißen"). "Sie" is also always capitalized in writing.

The Informal "du"

Use "du" when talking to:

  • Friends
  • Family members
  • Children and people much younger than you
  • Peers in casual situations (like at a university)

The verb form changes when you use "du" (e.g., "kommst", "heißt").

General Rule

When in doubt, always use the formal "Sie". It is polite to wait for the other person (especially if they are older or in a higher position) to offer the informal "du" to you. This is called "das Du anbieten".
