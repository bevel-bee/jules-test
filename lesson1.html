Special Characters (Sonderzeichen)

These characters are called "Umlaute" (for ä, ö, ü) and "Eszett" or "scharfes S" (for ß).

Letter Pronunciation Guide
Ä äPronounced like the 'a' in "cat" or "sad". It is like a short 'e' sound.
Ö öTo make this sound, say "ur" as in "fur", but keep your lips rounded.
Ü üTo make this sound, say "ee" as in "see", but keep your lips rounded.
ßThis is a sharp 's' sound, like "ss" in "street". It is often used after a long vowel. If you can't type it, you can write "ss" instead.

Greetings (Grüße)

Here are the most common ways to greet someone in German.

German English When to use
HalloHelloA general, all-purpose greeting. Can be used anytime.
Guten TagGood dayA more formal greeting, typically used from noon until evening.
Guten MorgenGood morningUsed in the morning, until about noon.
Guten AbendGood eveningUsed in the evening.

Saying Goodbye (Verabschiedung)

German English Notes
Auf WiedersehenGoodbyeThis is a formal way to say goodbye. It literally means "until we see again".
TschüssByeThis is a more informal and common way to say bye.
